# Retrospective: Epic 5 - The Intelligence Hub

**Date:** 2026-04-05
**Status:** Completed

## Epic Summary & Metrics

- **Completed Stories:** 4/4
- **Key Themes:** Strategic Incubation, Autonomous Research, Real-time Knowledge Curation.
- **Architectural Integrity:** Established the "Knowledge Layer" with multi-tenant research persistence and hot-loading workflows.

## Successes & Strengths

1.  **Strategic Agility:** The `IdeaForge` workspace allows the founder to instantly transition from brainstorming to operational activation, fulfilling the core goal of a self-evolving agency.
2.  **Autonomous Intelligence:** The `research-curator` workflow successfully transforms raw industrial text into structured, auditable records with automated confidence scoring.
3.  **High-Fidelity Interaction:** Leveraged Framer Motion `layout` and `AnimatePresence` to create cinematic transitions between hub modes and real-time vault synchronization.
4.  **Linguistic Consistency:** Enforced NZ English standard across all agent-generated summaries and system diagnostics (e.g., "Initialising", "Authorising").

## Challenges & Growth Areas

1.  **Type Safety in Dynamic Streams:** Merging hot-loaded modules with static projects in the HUD required a significant refactor of the `Project` interface to maintain TypeScript compliance.
2.  **State Synchronisation:** Initial hub polling was too aggressive; relaxed the interval to 15s and implemented `isMounted` guards to prevent memory leaks and race conditions.
3.  **Simulation vs Reality:** Like Epic 4, some external PDF ingestion remains simulated for Phase 1, requiring real-world tool-calling credentials for future expansion.

## Key Insights & Learnings

- **Mode-based Navigation:** Switching between "Research" and "Forge" modes within the same hub route provides a superior UX for founders who need to context-shift between analysis and creation.
- **Atomic Activation:** Hot-loading must be an atomic workflow step to prevent "Ghost Modules" where an idea is marked active but fails to appear in the operational HUD.
- **Industrial Precision:** Using Gemini 2.0 Flash for structured output (Zod) is the most reliable way to maintain consistent intelligence metadata across the agency.
